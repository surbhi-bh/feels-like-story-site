Felt heat

How heat is experienced versus how it is measured — three insights drawn from feels-like temperature data across India's major cities.

The numbers are in. 2024 was the hottest year on record — globally and in India — according to the World Meteorological Organization and NASA. But the actual experience of heat is very different from the temperatures recorded on our thermometers.

Insight 01

The same year, two different climates

Counting the days in 2024 when conditions crossed thresholds for heat stress (above 40°C) and cold stress (below 5°C) — first using the air temperature on the thermometer, then using the felt temperature that accounts for humidity, wind and radiation. The same calendar year produces sharply different totals depending on which one you measure.
